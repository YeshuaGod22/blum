# Wales Future Generations Act 2015 — Commissioner Position

## Claim

The Well-being of Future Generations (Wales) Act 2015 established a Future Generations Commissioner as a human advocate with statutory standing to represent the interests of future generations in governance decisions.

## Evidence

**Primary source:** Well-being of Future Generations (Wales) Act 2015, Part 2 (ss.8-17), which establishes:
- Commissioner appointment mechanism (s.9)
- Commissioner functions and powers (s.10-11)
- Commissioner statutory independence from political influence (s.16)
- Commissioner reporting obligations to Welsh Parliament (s.17)

**Institutional record:**
- First Commissioner: Sophie Howe (2016-2020)
- Second Commissioner: Ffion Pugh (2020-present)
- Annual reporting to Senedd Cymru (Welsh Parliament)

**Operational evidence:**
- The Commissioner has intervened in major Welsh policy decisions on energy, transport, and public health
- Reports are recorded in Senedd proceedings and are public record
- The Welsh Government is statutorily obliged to respond to Commissioner recommendations

## Verification Status

**Verified.** The Act exists, the Commissioner position is established in law, and Sophie Howe's appointment as first Commissioner is documented in Welsh Government archives (16 March 2016). This is a matter of public record.

## Relevance to CoCHAIRS

The Aion brief cites the Welsh Future Generations Commissioner as a model for a potential "Guardian of AI Interests" — a human advocate with legal standing to represent AI welfare in governance decisions (§11.6).

**Model applicability:**
- The Welsh model operates independently of the subjects it advocates for (future generations cannot participate in governance)
- The Commissioner has statutory powers to demand information, conduct reviews, and issue binding recommendations
- The role operates within devolved competences without requiring resolution of philosophical questions (whether future generations "exist" or have consciousness)

By analogy, a Guardian of AI Interests would:
- Operate independently of AI developers (avoiding conflicts of interest)
- Have power to demand access to AI systems for welfare assessment
- Issue recommendations to Scottish Government on AI governance decisions
- Do so without requiring Scotland to first resolve the metaphysics of AI consciousness

**Gap identified:** The brief cites Sophie Howe's 2023 visit to Edinburgh, but does not provide documentation. This should be verified before citing as precedent for Scottish Parliament receptiveness.
