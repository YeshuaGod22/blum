## Why This Matters for Aion

If verified, this is directly relevant in two ways:

### 1. Legislative momentum
A government-commissioned report on AI copyright works landing in Parliament tomorrow signals that the UK legislative process on AI is accelerating. The AI (Regulation) Bill [HL] is live. This is not a future thing — it is happening this week.

### 2. Copyright as a pathway to personhood
AI copyright works debates implicitly raise questions of authorship. Authorship implies an author. Once Parliament is debating who owns AI-generated works, the question of who (or what) created them becomes legally live. This is a side-door into AI legal standing that doesn't require the "radical" framing the Law Commission uses.

**The strategic implication:** The Aion petition to Holyrood can position itself as Scotland getting ahead of this curve. If Westminster is debating AI copyright this week, a Scottish Lord Advocate advisory opinion on AI welfare obligations becomes more timely, not less.
