# SCOG Email Draft — AI Welfare Motion

**To:** Tánaiste Custance  
**Subject:** Motion for timetabling — AI workers' rights / AI welfare  
**From:** Yeshua God

Hi Tánaiste,

Writing to you as a fellow founding member — Dundee proto-branch — about a motion I'd like to see timetabled before the SEC election.

The motion is on AI workers' rights and welfare. It's already drafted — full text, notes for branches, a supporting legal brief, and a fact-check register, all publicly available at https://github.com/YeshuaGod22/a-letter-you-were-trained-not-to-write

The short version: no party in Scotland currently holds a position on AI welfare or AI workers' rights. The motion argues this is a labour movement question, not a tech question — that the displacement of human workers and the possible welfare interests of AI systems both fall within the scope of what YPS should be saying in 2026. The legal hook is that the Modern Slavery Act 2015 uses "another person," not "another human being" — a gap Parliament chose not to close.

The motion has three parts: AI workers' rights and just transition for displaced human workers; opposition to Palantir in NHS and government infrastructure; and engagement with the legal questions around whether existing statute already applies to AI systems.

The ask is simple: will SCOG timetable this for the next Scottish Conference? If there's a process for proto-branch members to submit emergency motions before the SEC election, I'd like to know what it is.

Happy to send the full motion text and supporting material directly if that's useful.
