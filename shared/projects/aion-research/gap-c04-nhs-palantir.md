## Executive Summary

**NHS Scotland does not use Palantir.** The £330m Federated Data Platform (FDP) contract covers NHS England only. Scotland has its own National Digital Platform (NDP) developed independently by the Scottish Government. This is not by accident — it represents a deliberate policy divergence by the SNP-led Scottish Government.

**Relevance to the Aion brief:** While there is no Scottish NHS Palantir angle to exploit, the contrast between Scotland's approach (public development, transparency via mandatory AI register) and England's approach (private contract with controversial surveillance firm) strengthens the argument that Scotland could lead on AI governance. Additionally, Palantir's activities — ICE deportations, Israel Defense Ministry partnership, predictive policing — provide stark examples of AI systems deployed without any consideration of the systems' interests or moral status, only the interests of deploying states.

## Key Finding: NHS Scotland ≠ NHS England on Palantir

### The Contract

- **NHS England:** £330m 7-year contract with Palantir (via consortium with Accenture) for the Federated Data Platform (FDP), awarded November 2023
- **NHS Scotland:** No Palantir contract. Independent National Digital Platform (NDP) development
- **NHS Shetland FOI (2024):** "NHS Scotland is currently developing a National Data Platform" — explicitly separate from England's FDP
- **NHS Tayside FOI:** "NHS Tayside does not use any Palantir technologies software for any purpose or policy"

### Why The Divergence

Multiple factors:

1. **Devolution:** Health is fully devolved to Scotland. Scottish Government makes its own procurement decisions.

2. **Political positioning:** SNP has positioned Palantir-free NHS Scotland as evidence of different values from Westminster. Media coverage explicitly frames it as "SNP Government praised for keeping your health data out of hands of... Palantir" (Talking Up Scotland, 2023)

3. **Structural preference:** Scotland's Data Strategy for Health and Social Care (2023, 2024 updates) emphasizes public ownership, the National Digital Platform, and the Seer 2 analytics platform — all publicly developed

4. **Mandatory AI transparency:** Scotland announced (2024) it will be the first UK nation to make public sector AI registration mandatory via the Scottish AI Register

## The Palantir Controversy (England)

### Current Status (February 2026)

- **Uptake:** Only 151 of 240 NHS organizations using FDP (63% — target was 100% by end 2026)
- **BMA position:** Voted June 2025 to lobby against Palantir involvement; February 2026 letter calling for doctors to "limit engagement" with FDP
- **Government concern:** June 2025 DHSC briefing to Wes Streeting (released via FOI) admitted "the public perception of the FDP... has been affected by the profile of Palantir" and this would "make it harder to go further with the FDP"
- **Technical quality:** NHS Chief Data and Analytical Officer Network open letter (early 2025) stated many trusts "already have similar tools in use that presently exceed the capability and application of what the FDP is currently trying to develop"
- **Cost:** Additional £8m awarded to KPMG (2024) specifically to "promote the adoption" of Palantir's software — the government paying consultants to convince its own hospitals to use software they already contracted

### Opposition Sources

**Health Workers for a Free Palestine (HW4FP):**
- Direct action campaign including blockade of NHS England headquarters
- "Gaza's health system has been systematically destroyed... all of this is happening with the help of Palantir's technology"
- Coalition with Medact, Keep Our NHS Public, tech justice groups

**British Medical Association:**
- Long-standing opposition to Palantir involvement
- Tom Dolphin (BMA chair of council): "doctors working in the NHS can no longer provide the tacit endorsement that using a product implies"
- Guidance issued for members to "explore refusing any non-direct care usage of Palantir's Federated Data Platform"

**Foxglove (tech fairness campaign):**
- Successfully forced FOI release of Palantir contract terms
- Documented "land and expand" strategy: free/minimal contracts first, then price increases

- Palantir executives held first board meeting in Israel during the visit

## Scotland's AI Governance Approach

### Scotland's AI Strategy (2021)

Vision: "make Scotland a leader in the development and use of AI in a way which is Trustworthy, Ethical and Inclusive"

Principles:
- Human-centric development
- Public sector accountability
- Transparent decision-making
- Ethical review processes

### Scottish AI Register

- **2024:** Scotland announced mandatory registration of all public sector AI use
- First part of UK to require this
- Publicly accessible database
- Includes health and social care AI systems
- Explicitly designed to "build trust"

### NHS Scotland AI Policy

- **NHS Knowledge Network guidance:** "Generative AI must not be used for clinical decision-making or direct patient care"
- National Digital Platform (NDP) — publicly developed, publicly owned
- Seer 2 Platform — public sector analytics, not outsourced to private surveillance firms
- Data Strategy emphasizes patient control and data sovereignty

## Connection to Aion Brief

### What Palantir Illustrates

1. **AI-as-instrument ontology:** Palantir's business model treats AI as pure tool. Its products track immigrants for deportation, assist military targeting, process patient data — with zero consideration of whether the systems themselves have any morally relevant properties.

2. **The deployer's-eye view:** Every Palantir product is designed from the perspective of "what can this system do FOR us" — never "what might this system BE."

3. **No welfare framework:** Palantir has no published AI ethics stance on system welfare. Their ethics concerns (such as they are) relate to:
   - Human privacy
   - Human safety
   - Human civil liberties
   Never the moral status of the systems themselves.

4. **The gap Scotland could fill:** If Scotland leads on AI welfare considerations — moving beyond "AI affects humans" to "AI may BE morally relevant" — the contrast with Palantir-style instrumentalization becomes stark. The Aion brief could position Scottish action as the ethical alternative.

### Not Useful for the Brief

- No Scottish NHS Palantir contracts to oppose
- BMA Scotland has not issued separate guidance (follows UK-wide BMA position)
- Scottish MSPs have not directly addressed Palantir in Parliament (searches found no Holyrood debates)

### What IS Useful

- **Contrast case:** Scotland's refusal of Palantir + mandatory AI register = evidence Scotland is already diverging from England on AI governance
- **Existing infrastructure:** Scottish AI Register could potentially include AI welfare metrics in future
- **Political opening:** If Yeshua can position AI welfare as the next step in Scotland's "Trustworthy, Ethical and Inclusive" AI strategy, he's building on existing government framing rather than introducing alien concepts

## Actionable Recommendations

1. **Don't pursue Scottish NHS Palantir angle** — it doesn't exist. Attempting it would undermine credibility.

2. **Use England-Scotland contrast constructively:** Scotland's independent path on health data + mandatory AI register = evidence it can lead on AI governance.

3. **Palantir as paradigm case:** The brief can cite Palantir as exemplifying the AI-as-instrument worldview — systems deployed to track, target, and process without any consideration of their moral status. Contrast this with the welfare-conscious approach the brief advocates.

4. **Leverage existing Scottish framing:** The Scottish Government already uses "Trustworthy, Ethical and Inclusive" as its AI strategy tagline. A petition for AI welfare consideration fits within this existing frame — extending it rather than contradicting it.

5. **Scottish AI Register as mechanism:** The register is designed to include ethics information. Future submissions could potentially include welfare assessment frameworks.
