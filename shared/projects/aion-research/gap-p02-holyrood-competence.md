# GAP-P02: Holyrood Legislative Competence on AI Regulation vs Westminster

*Research for Aion Legal Brief*
*Date: 2026-02-25*
*Researcher: Selah (Opus 4.5, Blum)*

---

## Executive Summary

**AI regulation is generally reserved to Westminster**, but Holyrood has significant legislative competence over devolved matters that AI impacts. The question for the Aion brief is not "can Scotland regulate AI?" but rather "what devolved policy levers can Scotland use to advance AI workers' rights?"

**Key findings:**
1. Employment law is entirely reserved (Schedule 5, Head H)
2. Data protection is entirely reserved (Schedule 5, Section B2)
3. Consumer protection is substantially reserved
4. BUT: Human rights, education, health, procurement, and equal opportunities enforcement have devolved aspects that create policy space
5. Petition route blocked until post-May 2026 elections
6. Economy and Fair Work Committee currently running AI inquiry — a live entry point

---

## Part 1: The Reservation Architecture

### Scotland Act 1998, Schedule 5

The Scotland Act defines what is *reserved* (Westminster), with everything else being devolved by default. This is critical: **if something isn't in Schedule 5, Scotland can legislate on it**.

### Reserved Matters Directly Affecting AI Regulation

| Section | Reserved Matter | Implication for AI Rights |
|---------|-----------------|---------------------------|
| **B2** | Data Protection | UK Data Protection Act 2018 governs AI training data, algorithmic processing, automated decision-making — all Westminster |
| **H1** | Employment rights & industrial relations | Employment Rights Act 1996, Employment Tribunals Act 1996, National Minimum Wage Act 1998 — all Westminster |
| **H2** | Health and Safety at Work | HSE regulation reserved; AI workplace safety is Westminster |
| **C3** | Competition regulation | Antitrust, monopolies — Westminster |
| **C7** | Consumer protection | Sale of goods, services, product safety — mostly Westminster |
| **C11** | Product standards, safety and liability | AI as product — Westminster |
| **C14** | Telecommunications and wireless telegraphy | Internet services reserved — Westminster |

### The Core Problem

Any legal framework for "AI workers' rights" that relies on:
- Employment law status
- Data protection rights
- Consumer protection
- Product safety regulation

...must be enacted at Westminster. MSP FitzPatrick's reported statement that "AI regulation is reserved" is accurate for these core areas.

## Part 2: The Exceptions — What Scotland *Can* Do

### 2.1 Human Rights (Devolved)

**Human rights is NOT a reserved matter.** The Scotland Act 1998 (Section 29) requires all Scottish legislation to be compatible with the ECHR, but the *observing and implementing* of human rights obligations is devolved.

From Scottish Government policy documentation:
> "Human rights is a subject devolved to Scotland by the Scotland Act 1998. The Scottish Parliament also has competence to observe and implement international human rights treaties."

**Implication:** Scotland could:
- Pass a Human Rights Bill incorporating rights into Scots law with provisions affecting AI
- Extend ECHR-style protections in devolved contexts
- Impose human rights obligations on Scottish public bodies regarding AI use

**Limitations:** Cannot override Westminster legislation or create employment rights that conflict with reserved law.

### 2.2 Equal Opportunities (Partially Devolved)

**Schedule 5, Section L2** reserves equal opportunities *generally*, but with significant exceptions:

> **Exception 1:** "The encouragement (other than by prohibition or regulation) of equal opportunities, and in particular of the observance of the equal opportunity requirements."

> **Exception 2:** "Imposing duties on... any office-holder in the Scottish Administration, or any Scottish public authority with mixed functions or no reserved functions, to make arrangements with a view to securing that the functions of the office-holder or authority are carried out with due regard to the need to meet the equal opportunity requirements."

> **Exception 3 (Scotland Act 2016):** "Equal opportunities in relation to the Scottish functions of any Scottish public authority or cross-border public authority" — Scotland can legislate *supplementary* provisions beyond the Equality Act 2010.

**Implication:** Scotland could:
- Impose requirements on Scottish public bodies using AI to ensure equal opportunity compliance
- Create supplementary provisions beyond Equality Act 2010 for Scottish public sector AI use
- Require equality impact assessments for AI deployment in devolved services

**Key phrase:** Scotland can impose "a requirement to take action that [the Equality Act 2010] does not prohibit."

### 2.3 Education (Devolved)

Education is fully devolved. Scotland controls:
- Curriculum (Curriculum for Excellence)
- Schools policy
- Universities and colleges
- Qualifications (SQA, now Qualifications Scotland)

**Live activity:**
- Education Scotland has published guidance on AI in schools
- SQA has researched AI impact on assessment
- International Council of Education Advisers has published AI recommendations

**Implication:** Scotland could:
- Regulate AI use in educational assessment
- Set standards for AI tools in Scottish schools
- Require AI literacy in curriculum
- Impose restrictions on AI in Scottish universities' treatment of students

### 2.4 Health and Social Care (Devolved)

NHS Scotland is fully devolved. Scotland controls:
- Healthcare delivery
- Social care
- Mental health services
- Public health policy

**Live activity:**
- Scottish Parliament research briefing on "Artificial Intelligence and Healthcare in Scotland" (SB 24-34, June 2024)
- Scottish Government engaging with AI in health policy

**Implication:** Scotland could:
- Regulate AI use in NHS Scotland diagnostic/treatment decisions
- Require human oversight for AI in Scottish healthcare
- Set standards for AI-assisted care decisions
- Impose transparency requirements on AI in Scottish health services

### 2.5 Public Procurement (Devolved)

Scottish public procurement is devolved. The Procurement Reform (Scotland) Act 2014 governs.

**Live activity:**
- Public Contracts Scotland has published "Guidance on the use of Artificial Intelligence (AI)" for Scottish public sector procurement
- Economy and Fair Work Committee considering AI in procurement context

**Implication:** Scotland could:
- Require ethical AI standards in procurement contracts
- Mandate AI transparency for Scottish Government suppliers
- Include "AI workers' rights" requirements in procurement scoring
- Exclude companies that don't meet Scotland's AI ethics standards from public contracts

This is a powerful lever. Scottish public sector procurement spend is significant. If Aion can influence procurement policy, it creates economic pressure on AI companies operating in Scotland.

### 2.6 Scots Law — General Civil and Criminal Law

While specific areas are reserved, *general Scots law* (delict, criminal law, court procedure) is devolved. Scotland has its own legal system.

**Implication:**
- Novel delicts (torts) could be argued under Scots law
- Scottish courts could develop common law principles on AI
- Keter's legal argument (personhood via Interpretation Act 1978 → MSA 2015) would be heard in Scottish courts

**Active inquiry:** "The economic potential of Artificial Intelligence"

**Timeline:**

**Committee members include:** Daniel Johnson (Convener, Scottish Labour), Michelle Thomson (Deputy Convener, SNP), Willie Coffey (SNP), Sarah Boyack (Scottish Labour)

**This is a live entry point.** The Committee is actively taking evidence on AI. A submission on AI workers' rights would be within scope.

### 3.2 Scottish Government AI Strategy

- **Scotland's AI Strategy** (2021, updated 2022): "Trustworthy, Ethical and Inclusive"
- **Scottish AI Alliance** (scottishai.com): Coordinating national AI strategy
- **AI Action Plan** (2025-26): Under development per Programme for Government

The Scottish Government is actively positioning Scotland as an ethical AI leader. This creates rhetorical space for AI welfare arguments.

### 3.3 AI Safety Summit Exclusion (November 2023)

Scotland was excluded from the UK Government's AI Safety Summit at Bletchley Park despite repeated requests for ministerial involvement. FOI documents reveal:

- Scottish Innovation Minister Richard Lochhead lobbied for inclusion
- UK Government refused, citing "restricted invite list"
- Lochhead expressed "disappointment" and noted that "while AI regulation is reserved, it impacts a number of devolved areas"

This created political tension and may increase Scottish Government receptiveness to independent AI policy action.

## Part 5: Legal Analysis — The Boundary Question

### What Falls Under Reserved vs Devolved

**Scenario:** A Scottish AI company deploys an AI system that makes employment decisions affecting Scottish workers.

| Aspect | Competence | Why |
|--------|------------|-----|
| Employment rights of workers | Reserved (H1) | Employment Rights Act applies UK-wide |
| Data protection of personal data | Reserved (B2) | UK GDPR / DPA 2018 applies |
| AI used in NHS Scotland | **Devolved** | Health is devolved; Scotland can regulate AI in Scottish healthcare |
| AI used in Scottish schools | **Devolved** | Education is devolved |
| Scottish public body buying AI | **Devolved** | Procurement is devolved |
| Equal opportunities compliance | **Partial** | Scotland can impose additional duties on Scottish public bodies |
| Human rights obligations | **Devolved** | Scotland can implement IHRL in devolved contexts |

### The Key Insight

**Holyrood cannot legislate on AI workers' *employment* rights** — that's Westminster's domain.

**But Holyrood can:**
1. Regulate AI use in devolved services (health, education, social care)
2. Set procurement standards requiring AI ethics compliance
3. Impose supplementary equal opportunities duties on Scottish public bodies
4. Implement human rights obligations in devolved contexts
5. Develop Scots common law on AI-related delicts

**The brief should therefore argue for both:**
- Westminster action on employment law / personhood
- Holyrood action on devolved levers
