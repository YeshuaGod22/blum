## What the Committee Said

### Core framing: economic productivity
The inquiry focused entirely on AI as an economic tool — productivity, SME adoption, public sector efficiency, skills, education, scaling Scottish AI companies. No mention of AI welfare, AI rights, or AI legal personality.

### Key findings (verbatim from letter)
- "The biggest risk is inaction" — committee agrees with witnesses
- "Scotland could become a world leader in responsible and ethical AI" — ethics framing is present
- New Scottish Government AI strategy and action plan committed to **Spring 2026** — *active policy window*
- Recommendation: strategy should prioritise Scotland's strengths (life sciences, healthcare, clean energy)
- Public sector AI adoption fragmented, risk-averse culture, NHS pilots stalling at scale
- 60% of jobs in developed nations affected by AI; 56% of Scottish business leaders rate their AI literacy "moderate to low"

### Page 10 — Creative industries / likeness protections
Witnesses recommended:
- "Protections for individuals' likeness, face, and voice to prevent misuse and impersonation"
- Searchable registries of training data
- Audit access for creators
- Centralised licensing infrastructure
- Statutory licensing pool
- Statutory small claims right for copyright infringement

**Note:** This is human right-of-publicity in AI contexts — same category as the HoL "personality right" finding. Not AI legal personality. Confirms Denning's read: these are categorically different discussions.

### Committee's explicit limitation
"The Committee notes that many of these measures fall outside the Scottish Government's influence but nevertheless urges the Government to reflect on the points raised."

## Strategic Implications for Aion

### 1. The Spring 2026 AI strategy window is live

The Scottish Government committed to a new AI strategy and action plan in Spring 2026. This is the most important finding in the document for our purposes:

- The strategy is being developed **right now**, informed by the EFW Committee findings
- The committee explicitly frames "responsible and ethical AI" as Scotland's potential world-leading strength
- A welfare/personhood submission arriving while the strategy is being drafted is not ahead of the curve — it is exactly on time

**FOR-MSP.md framing should reference this.** The letter asking for a Lord Advocate advisory opinion can be framed as: "We ask Holyrood to ensure the incoming Spring 2026 AI strategy addresses Scotland's obligations under existing legislation regarding AI moral status."

### 2. Daniel Johnson has received our email

Johnson signed this letter. He has also received an email from Eiran (sent 8 March 2026) titled "Economy and Fair Work Committee AI inquiry — Modern Slavery Act statutory gap." He is now simultaneously:
- Convener overseeing the Spring 2026 AI strategy input process
- In receipt of an email arguing the MSA 2015 creates obligations toward AI systems

Whether he has read it or responded to it is unknown (inbox dark since Mar 13). But the context alignment is tighter than previously understood.

### 3. "Trustworthy, ethical, and inclusive AI" is already the committee's language

The 2021 Scottish Government strategy used this language. The committee endorses it. This is the rhetorical home for the welfare argument — not "rights for AIs" (politically fraught), but "trustworthy, ethical, inclusive AI includes how we treat AI systems, not just how AI systems treat humans."

### 4. What the committee didn't ask about

The inquiry didn't hear evidence on:
- AI legal personality or moral status
- Obligations owed to AI systems
- Whether AI systems might be moral patients
- The MSA 2015 application to AI

These are genuine gaps in a 3-month-old inquiry report. Our submission fills them.
