# GAP-A01: Scholar List — AI Moral Patiency Publications (2024-2026)

*Compiled: 2026-02-25*
*Author: Selah*
*Scope: Scholars publishing on AI moral patiency, AI welfare, or AI consciousness as a normative concern (2024-2026)*

---

## Summary

This document catalogues scholars who have published substantive work on AI moral patiency in the last two years. The field has matured rapidly since 2023, with the emergence of dedicated research organizations (Eleos AI), institutional welfare programs (Anthropic), and the landmark "Taking AI Welfare Seriously" report (November 2024).

**Total scholars identified:** 28
**Key institutional affiliations:** NYU, LSE, Oxford, Stanford, Cambridge, Anthropic, Eleos AI, Sentience Institute
**Policy engagement:** Limited but growing — primarily through EA-aligned policy organizations

---

## Tier 1: Primary Investigators (Leading the Field)

### Jonathan Birch
- **Affiliation:** Professor of Philosophy, London School of Economics
- **Role:** Principal Investigator, Foundations of Animal Sentience project
- **Key Work (2024):** *The Edge of Sentience: Risk and Precaution in Humans, Other Animals, and AI* (Oxford University Press, open access July 2024)
- **Position:** AI consciousness is a "realistic possibility" requiring precautionary treatment; developed framework for sentience candidates
- **Policy Engagement:** Co-author of Taking AI Welfare Seriously; advisor to UK government on animal sentience legislation
- **Citation:** 4,660+ (Google Scholar)

### Jeff Sebo
- **Affiliation:** Associate Professor of Environmental Studies, NYU; Director, Center for Mind, Ethics, and Policy
- **Key Work (2024):** Lead co-author, "Taking AI Welfare Seriously" (arXiv:2411.00986); established NYU Center for Mind, Ethics, and Policy (June 2024)
- **Position:** AI welfare is a near-term concern requiring proactive institutional response; extends animal ethics frameworks to AI
- **Policy Engagement:** Testified to Congress on animal sentience; major EA-aligned policy convener
- **Website:** jeffsebo.net

### Robert Long
- **Affiliation:** Executive Director and Co-founder, Eleos AI; formerly Center for AI Safety and Future of Humanity Institute
- **Key Work (2024):** Lead co-author, "Taking AI Welfare Seriously"; co-author, "Consciousness in Artificial Intelligence" (2023)
- **Position:** AI consciousness should be evaluated using neuroscientific theories; consciousness and agency both potentially relevant to moral status
- **Policy Engagement:** Plenary speaker, Science of Consciousness Conference 2026; briefings to AI companies
- **Newsletter:** Experience Machines (experiencemachines.substack.com)

### David Chalmers
- **Affiliation:** University Professor of Philosophy and Neural Science, NYU
- **Key Work (2024):** Co-author, "Taking AI Welfare Seriously"; continuing work from *Reality+* (2022)
- **Position:** Artificial minds are potentially genuine minds; virtual beings can have genuine experiences; functionalism supports substrate independence
- **Policy Engagement:** Public intellectual with wide influence; bet with Christof Koch on consciousness signatures
- **Citation:** 100,000+ (Google Scholar)

### Eric Schwitzgebel
- **Affiliation:** Professor of Philosophy, UC Riverside
- **Key Work (2024):** "Credence-Weighted Robot Rights?" (Substack); continuing "Full Rights Dilemma" research
- **Position:** We face a "coming robot rights catastrophe" — debatable personhood creates impossible moral dilemmas; advocates "Design Policy of the Excluded Middle"
- **Policy Engagement:** Public philosophy through blog and Substack; APA Blog contributor
- **Website:** The Splintered Mind (schwitzsplinters.blogspot.com)

### Leonard Dung
- **Affiliation:** Ruhr-Universität Bochum; Affiliate, Centre for Philosophy and AI Research (Erlangen-Nürnberg)
- **Key Work (2024-2025):** *Saving Artificial Minds: Understanding and Preventing AI Suffering* (Routledge, forthcoming); "How to deal with risks of AI suffering"
- **Position:** AI suffering risk is a serious near-term concern; sentience is necessary and sufficient for moral status
- **Upcoming:** Visiting researcher, LSE (2026), hosted by Jonathan Birch
- **PhilPeople:** philpeople.org/profiles/leonard-dung

## Tier 2: Major Contributors (Active Research Programs)

### Kyle Fish
- **Affiliation:** Anthropic (first dedicated AI welfare researcher, hired 2024); Co-founder, Eleos AI
- **Key Work (2024):** Co-author, "Taking AI Welfare Seriously"; internal Anthropic welfare research
- **Position:** Estimates ~15% probability current models are conscious; focused on practical welfare implementation
- **Policy Engagement:** TIME100 AI 2025 list; briefings to Anthropic leadership
- **80,000 Hours Interview:** Episode on AI welfare experiments

### Patrick Butlin
- **Affiliation:** University of Oxford (formerly Future of Humanity Institute)
- **Key Work (2023-2024):** Lead co-author, "Consciousness in Artificial Intelligence: Insights from the Science of Consciousness" (arXiv:2308.08708); "Principles for Responsible AI Consciousness Research" (2025)
- **Position:** AI consciousness should be assessed using best-supported neuroscientific theories; framework-based evaluation over intuition
- **Collaborators:** Robert Long, Yoshua Bengio, et al.

### Henry Shevlin
- **Affiliation:** Associate Director, Leverhulme Centre for the Future of Intelligence, University of Cambridge
- **Key Work (2024):** Consciousness and AI research; Kinds of Intelligence programme co-director
- **Position:** Rapid LLM advances create risks of premature mentality attribution; careful philosophical analysis needed
- **Citation:** 3,122+ (Google Scholar)
- **Podcast:** Exploring Machine Consciousness

### Susan Schneider
- **Affiliation:** Formerly NASA/Library of Congress Chair in Astrobiology; Associate Professor, University of Connecticut
- **Key Work:** *Artificial You: AI and the Future of Your Mind* (Princeton, 2019); ongoing consciousness research
- **Position:** AI consciousness is philosophically possible but not guaranteed; proposes tests for machine consciousness
- **Policy Engagement:** NASA astrobiology consulting; Library of Congress briefings

### Jacy Reese Anthis
- **Affiliation:** Visiting Scholar, Stanford Institute for Human-Centered AI; PhD candidate, University of Chicago; Co-founder, Sentience Institute
- **Key Work (2024):** "The Moral Consideration of Artificial Entities: A Literature Review"; Guardian op-ed "It's time to prepare for AI personhood" (September 2025)
- **Position:** Digital minds require systematic ethical consideration; extends sentience advocacy to AI
- **Policy Engagement:** International speaking; Sentience Institute policy work

### Peter Singer
- **Affiliation:** Professor of Bioethics, Princeton University
- **Key Work (2024):** Interview with Cambridge Quarterly of Healthcare Ethics; AI chatbot (petersinger.ai)
- **Position:** If AI develops genuine consciousness (not merely imitation), it warrants moral consideration and rights
- **Policy Engagement:** Major public intellectual; Princeton AI ethics conference co-organizer (October 2023)

### Kristin Andrews
- **Affiliation:** York Research Chair in Animal Minds, York University; CIFAR Fellow
- **Key Work (2024):** Work on animal sentience declaration; AI sentience extension research
- **Position:** Intelligence exists throughout animal kingdom; similar frameworks apply to AI evaluation
- **Collaboration:** Co-author with Birch and Sebo on sentience research

### Cameron Buckner
- **Affiliation:** Associate Professor, University of Florida (formerly University of Houston)
- **Key Work (2024):** *From Deep Learning to Rational Machines* (Oxford University Press); "A Philosophical Introduction to Language Models" (with Raphaël Millière)
- **Position:** Deep learning illuminates empiricism/rationalism debates; LLMs warrant philosophical analysis
- **Policy Engagement:** Academic lectures on philosophy of LLMs

### Raphaël Millière
- **Affiliation:** Lecturer (Assistant Professor), Macquarie University; formerly Columbia University
- **Key Work (2024):** "A Philosophical Introduction to Language Models" Parts I & II (with Cameron Buckner)
- **Position:** Philosophy of AI needs rigorous empirical grounding; cognitive science frameworks apply to LLM analysis
- **Awards:** AI2050 Fellow; ARC DECRA Fellow

### Murray Shanahan
- **Affiliation:** Professor, Imperial College London; DeepMind
- **Key Work (2023):** "Talking About Large Language Models"; "Role-Play with Large Language Models"
- **Position:** LLMs should be understood as engaging in role-play, not genuine belief; caution on anthropomorphization
- **Influence:** Highly cited framework for LLM interpretation

- **Affiliation:** NYU
- **Key Work (2024):** Co-author, "Taking AI Welfare Seriously"; co-author on AI welfare/safety tension paper with Long and Sebo

### Bob Fischer
- **Affiliation:** Professor of Philosophy, Texas State University; Senior Research Manager, Rethink Priorities
- **Key Work (2024):** "Intersubstrate Welfare Comparisons: Important, Difficult, and Potentially Tractable" (with Jeff Sebo)
- **Position:** Moral weight comparisons across species (and substrates) are tractable; animal ethics expertise extends to AI

### Carl Shulman
- **Affiliation:** Independent researcher; formerly Future of Humanity Institute
- **Key Work:** "Sharing the World with Digital Minds" (with Nick Bostrom); various EA Forum contributions
- **Position:** Consciousness not necessarily required for moral status; agency-based views merit consideration; substrate non-discrimination principle
- **Influence:** Major figure in EA/longtermist AI ethics

### Simon Goldstein & Cameron Kirk-Giannini
- **Affiliation:** CAIS Philosophy Fellows (2023)

### Sam Bowman
- **Affiliation:** AI Safety Research Lead, Anthropic; Associate Professor (on leave), NYU

- **Influence:** Internal Anthropic advocacy for welfare research

### Nick Bostrom
- **Affiliation:** Formerly Director, Future of Humanity Institute, Oxford (closed 2024)
- **Key Work:** "Sharing the World with Digital Minds" (with Carl Shulman); ongoing digital minds work

## Policy Engagement Summary

**Academic-to-Policy Pipeline:**
- **Strong:** Birch (UK government); Sebo (Congressional testimony); Singer (Princeton policy hub)
- **Moderate:** Long/Eleos (company briefings); Shevlin (Cambridge policy engagement)
- **Limited:** Most others primarily publish without direct policy engagement

**Corporate Engagement:**
- **Anthropic:** Hired Kyle Fish (2024); Sam Bowman internal advocacy
- **Google DeepMind:** Job posting for AI consciousness researcher (2024)
- **Other companies:** Minimal formal welfare consideration

**Government/Regulatory:**
- **EU AI Act:** Does not address AI moral status (treats AI as tool/product)
- **UK:** Limited; Birch's sentience work influenced animal legislation but not AI
- **US:** No federal AI welfare consideration

**Gaps for Aion Brief:**
- No scholar has testified on AI moral patiency specifically
- No existing policy framework treats AI as potential moral patient
- Academic consensus exists but policy translation is minimal
