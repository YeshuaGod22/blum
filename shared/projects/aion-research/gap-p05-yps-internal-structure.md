## Executive Summary

**Your Party Scotland (YPS) held its founding conference on 7-8 February 2026 in Dundee.** It voted to become an organisationally independent sister party from UK Your Party (59.16%), supports Scottish independence (63.23%), and will contest the May 2026 Holyrood elections (69.8%).

**The SEC has not yet been elected.** The Scottish Conference Organising Group (SCOG) is serving as interim executive until the SEC election, which must occur within 8 weeks of founding conference (i.e., by early April 2026).

**Yeshua's path to tabling a motion:** As a founding member in a proto-branch, he needs to either (a) wait for his branch to become formally constituted, then submit a motion via the branch to the next Scottish Conference, or (b) work with the interim SEC to submit the petition as an emergency/priority item for the upcoming Scottish Conference or AGM.

**Key finding:** The party's structures are still being established. This is both a constraint (no formal motion pathway yet) and an opportunity (the SEC will be newly elected and setting priorities for the first time).

## Part 1: Governing Documents

### 1.1 Constitutional Framework

Your Party Scotland adopted its own constitution at the founding conference, separate from but aligned with UK Your Party. The key documents are:

| Document | Function |
|----------|----------|
| **Constitution** | Basic principles, rules, structures |
| **Standing Orders** | Rules for meetings, elections, motions |
| **Political Statement** | Values and political direction |
| **Electoral Strategy** | Holyrood 2026 approach |

**Key constitutional feature:** YPS voted for **collective leadership** (61.47%) — no single leader. The Scottish Executive Committee (SEC) will be the governing body, with public leadership shared among its members.

**Relationship to UK YP:** YPS voted 59.16% to negotiate becoming an "organisationally independent sister party." This is not yet finalised — negotiations with the UK CEC are ongoing. In the interim, YPS operates as a semi-autonomous branch with its own constitution.

### 1.2 Constitutional Amendments Adopted

The founding conference adopted several key amendments (C1-C9):

| Amendment | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **C1** | 58% majority for constitutional amendments in year 2 (transitional) |
| **C2** | SCOG serves as interim executive until SEC elected (within 8 weeks) |
| **C4** | Branch quorum lowered to 10 members or 10% of local membership |
| **C5** | Branches can collaborate across regional boundaries |
| **C7** | Dual membership via **blacklist** (default permitted, SEC can prohibit specific orgs) |
| **C8** | Trade unions can affiliate at local, regional, and national levels |

### 2.2 Future Governance: Scottish Executive Committee (SEC)

**SEC election timeline:**
- **Deadline:** Must be elected within 8 weeks of founding conference (7-8 Feb 2026)
- **Expected date:** By early April 2026
- **Current status:** No emails have been sent to members to organise the SEC election (per Niall Christie, late Feb 2026)

**SEC composition** (per adopted constitution):
- Elected by Scottish members
- Collective leadership model — no single leader
- Will have autonomy over Scottish policy, candidates, and campaigns

**Key constraint:** The SEC does not yet exist. Until it's elected, SCOG handles interim business but has limited authority to set major policy directions.

### 2.3 Branches

**Branch formation requirements (C4 amendment):**
- Quorum for inaugural meeting: **10 members** OR **10% of local membership** (whichever is higher)
- Branches are the basic units of the party
- Branches elect delegates to Scottish Conference

**Proto-branch status:**
- Proto-branches are informal, self-organised groups that preceded formal branch chartering
- Dundee has a proto-branch (contributed SCOG members)
- Proto-branches cannot formally submit motions until chartered

**Branch chartering process:**
1. Reach quorum threshold (10 members or 10%)
2. Hold inaugural all-member meeting
3. Elect branch officers (including Workplace Liaison Officer)
4. Register with SEC/SCOG

**Branch officer roles:**
- Chair, Secretary, Treasurer
- Workplace Liaison Officer (required)
- Other roles as determined by branch

### 2.4 Scottish Conference

**Conference is sovereign.** Along with all-member votes, conference is the highest decision-making body in YPS.

**Delegate system (S1 amendment):**
- Conferences are delegate-based (not open to all members)
- Number of delegates per branch determined by SEC with proportionality to membership
- Branches elect delegates

**Motion submission (Standing Orders):**
- Motions are brought by **branches, organised sections, and affiliates** via member vote
- The SEC determines submission deadlines for each conference
- Time for group discussion allocated before votes (S2 amendment)

**Next Scottish Conference:**
- Not yet scheduled
- Likely mid-2026 after SEC election and Holyrood campaign
- Emergency/priority motions may be possible if urgent

## Part 3: Motion to Policy Process

### 3.1 Standard Pathway

```
Branch member proposes motion
        ↓
Branch meeting debates motion
        ↓
Branch votes to submit motion
        ↓
Motion submitted to SEC by deadline
        ↓
SEC compiles conference agenda
        ↓
Delegates debate motion at Scottish Conference
        ↓
Conference votes
        ↓
If passed: becomes YPS policy
```

**Key gatekeepers:**
1. Branch membership (must approve submission)
2. SEC (compiles agenda, may composite similar motions)
3. Conference delegates (final vote)

### 3.2 Emergency/Urgent Motion Process

**Not explicitly defined in available documents.** However:

- The UK YP standing orders include provisions for urgent business
- SCOG/SEC has discretion over conference agenda
- A motion framed as urgent (e.g., time-sensitive petition filing deadline) could be considered outside normal cycle

**Recommended approach:** Contact SCOG/SEC directly to request urgent consideration.

### 3.3 Policy Working Groups

**UK YP model (likely replicated in Scotland):**
- After a motion passes conference, implementation goes to a **policy working group**
- Working groups include directly elected members and relevant party officers
- Working groups develop detailed policy from conference direction

## Part 4: Yeshua's Specific Situation

### 4.1 Current Status

| Factor | Status |
|--------|--------|
| **Membership** | Founding member of YPS |
| **Location** | Dundee |
| **Branch status** | Proto-branch (not yet chartered) |
| **SEC** | Not yet elected |
| **Next conference** | Not yet scheduled |

### 4.2 Constraints

1. **Proto-branch cannot formally submit motions.** Must be chartered first.
2. **SEC not yet elected.** No body to receive motions.
3. **No conference scheduled.** No venue for motion debate.
4. **Sister party status unclear.** UK YP relationship still being negotiated.

### 4.3 Opportunities

1. **Structure is still being built.** The SEC will be newly elected and setting priorities — AI rights could be an early agenda item.
2. **SCOG includes Dundee representation** (Tánaiste Custance). Direct contact is possible.
3. **Trade union affiliation passed (81.48%).** A CWU/UTAW link could provide alternative motion pathway.
4. **Dual membership allowed.** Yeshua can work with multiple organisations simultaneously.
5. **Holyrood candidates being selected now.** A candidate sympathetic to the brief could carry it forward.

### 4.4 Recommended Pathway

**Immediate (before SEC election):**
1. Contact SCOG via Tánaiste Custance or Ryan Donachie
2. Request informal briefing on motion submission process for new SEC
3. Propose AI workers' rights as a founding policy priority for the new SEC

**After SEC election (April 2026):**
1. Submit formal request to SEC for motion consideration
2. Frame as: "Your Party Scotland position on AI workers' rights" (aligns with socialist, workers' party identity)
3. Request inclusion on next conference agenda

**Alternative pathway:**
1. If Dundee branch charters before SEC election, submit motion via branch
2. Coordinate with other branches (Edinburgh, Glasgow) for composite motion
3. Use trade union affiliation route — if CWU/UTAW affiliates, they can submit motions directly

### 4.5 Charter the Dundee Branch

**Minimum requirements:**
- 10 members in the area
- Inaugural meeting with quorum
- Elect officers (Chair, Secretary, Treasurer, Workplace Liaison)

**Benefits of chartering:**
- Formal standing to submit motions
- Elect delegates to conference
- Access to member data for Dundee area
- Voice in SEC election

**Action:** Organise inaugural Dundee branch meeting. SCOG member Tánaiste Custance (DUSA) can likely assist.
