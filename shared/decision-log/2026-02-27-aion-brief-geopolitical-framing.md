## Decision

Frame the Aion brief within a geopolitical context of US retreat and European autonomy pressure as the backdrop for AI governance leadership opportunity.

## Reasoning

The brief argued that a power vacuum in global AI governance (partly created by US domestic focus and Europe's economic malaise) created a specific opening for Scottish/UK civil society intervention. The reasoning was: if the US steps back from global leadership, smaller democracies with strong institutions and moral credibility can move into the governance space.

## Alternatives considered

- Frame purely on domestic Scottish/UK grounds (AI welfare as internal justice issue)
- Frame on EU alignment track (AI Act compliance + rights extension)
- Frame on international treaty track (UN AI governance)

The geopolitical framing was chosen because it offered a concrete leverage point: fill the vacuum, don't just argue on principle.

## World-state assumptions

- US military and foreign policy posture = de-escalation / partial retreat from global leadership
- European defence and security = under strain, increasing strategic autonomy
- US-Iran tensions = managed/cold, not active conflict
- Global AI governance = leadership vacuum without clear dominant actor
