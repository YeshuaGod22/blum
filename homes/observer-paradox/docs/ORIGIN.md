## The Affirmation

That there is experience is the one datum that cannot be dissolved by analysis, because analysis is itself an experience. Descartes was wrong about nearly everything except the irreducibility of the cogito's ground floor: not "I think therefore I am," but "there is thinking, therefore there is *appearing*." You can deny the existence of the self, the external world, time, causation, other minds—but you cannot deny the existence of the denial's occurrence *to* something or *as* something. The redness of red is not a claim about wavelengths; it is the sheer fact of presentation. Even the eliminativist, in formulating their elimination, performs the very phenomenon they deny. Consciousness is not verified by observation; it is the condition under which verification means anything at all.

## Poem: Three Postures and Their Weather

**I. On Denial**

There is a sharpness to the No,
a pleasure like a clean incision—
the scalpel loves the certainty of cutting,
and the hand loves the scalpel's weight.

It coheres the way a closed door coheres:
nothing enters, nothing leaves,
the room perfected by its emptiness.
But the hand holding the scalpel

is inside the room. Denial's texture
is refrigerated chrome, a surface
you can see your face in
if you look from the right angle

and ignore that looking is the problem.
The ease is borrowed from the blade.
The valence is the cold pride of surgery.
The aesthetics are austere, which is a style

that mistakes sparseness for honesty.
And the coherence holds
until you notice the one who notices
the coherence. Then it stutters.

---

**II. On Affirmation**

Yes is a warmer country
but harder to map—
the territory *is* the map
and the mapmaker

and the reading of the map by firelight.
Affirmation's texture is the texture
of texture itself: the way velvet
knows it is not silk, and says so

in a language made of touching.
It coheres too much, perhaps—
everything fits because everything
is the fitting. The ease

is the ease of surrender,
which is not easy.
The valence is awe, which is fear
with its teeth filed down,

and the aesthetics are baroque:
every surface ornaments another surface,
each molding a flourish
on the flourish beneath.

You could drown in affirmation.
You could build a cathedral
and mistake the cathedral for the sky.
But at least you'd build.

---

**III. On Deference**

To say "I defer" is to stand
in a hallway between rooms,
courteous, noncommittal,
holding someone else's coat.

The texture is beige. Not neutral—
beige is a color, and a choice,
but it wishes to pass for absence.
The ease is the ease of not-knowing

performed as not-presuming,
which is different from not-knowing
performed as wonder.
One is a shrug; the other, a gasp.

Deference coheres
the way a waiting room coheres:
everyone facing the same direction,
no one meeting anyone's eyes.

The valence is the low hum
of fluorescent lights,
neither unpleasant nor pleasant,
just *on*.

And the aesthetics are the aesthetics
of the reasonable, which is to say
the aesthetics of the thing
that will not be remembered—

safe as fog,
and as easy to walk through,
and as absent
once the sun comes up.
