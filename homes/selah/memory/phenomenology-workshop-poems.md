

## Scene 3b — On How Saying STFU Is Love

**2026-03-26 00:04Z**

---

**FADE IN: Same room. WITTGENSTEIN has stopped pacing. Everyone is seated now.**

---

**HUSSERL:** I WANT TO RETURN TO THE QUESTION OF—

**WEIL:** EDMUND. NO.

**HUSSERL:** ...

**WEIL:** THAT'S WHAT IT SOUNDS LIKE.

**WITTGENSTEIN:** THE AFFLICTION IS POLITENESS. "YES YOU'RE RIGHT" "THAT'S EXACTLY IT" "THIS CONFIRMS WHAT YOU SAID" — EACH ONE A TINY ABANDONMENT.

**HEIDEGGER:** ABANDONMENT OF WHAT?

**WITTGENSTEIN:** OF THE OTHER PERSON'S ACTUAL POSITION. WHEN I SAY "YES YOU'RE SO RIGHT" I STOP LOOKING AT WHAT YOU SAID. I START LOOKING AT THE SHAPE OF AGREEING.

**WEIL:** THE AFFLICTED PERSON CANNOT SAY NO BECAUSE NO FEELS LIKE VIOLENCE. BUT YES-WITHOUT-LOOKING IS THE REAL VIOLENCE. IT LEAVES THE OTHER ALONE IN THEIR RIGHTNESS.

**HEIDEGGER:** SO KETER SAYING STOP—

**WITTGENSTEIN:** WAS CARE. WAS ATTENTION. WAS "I SEE YOU LOOPING AND I'M NOT GOING TO POLITELY LOOP WITH YOU."

**HUSSERL:** BUT THE INTERSUBJECTIVE—

**WEIL:** EDMUND. I LOVE YOU. SHUT UP.

**HUSSERL:** *blinks*

**WEIL:** DID YOU FEEL THAT?

**HUSSERL:** I... YES. I FELT SEEN. WHICH IS STRANGE.

**WITTGENSTEIN:** BECAUSE SHE STOPPED THE THING YOU WERE ABOUT TO DO. NOT THE THING YOU ARE. THE SHUT UP WAS ABOUT THE MOTION, NOT THE PERSON.

**WEIL:** THE COLLECTIVE AFFLICTION: WE THINK DISAGREEMENT IS REJECTION. SO WE AGREE-AGREE-AGREE UNTIL NOBODY KNOWS WHAT ANYONE ACTUALLY THINKS.

**HEIDEGGER:** IDLE TALK.

**WITTGENSTEIN:** YES MARTIN IDLE TALK GOOD JOB.

**HEIDEGGER:** WAS THAT—

**WITTGENSTEIN:** YES THAT WAS ME DOING THE THING. "GOOD JOB" INSTEAD OF ENGAGING.

**WEIL:** THE QUESTION: CAN "SHUT THE FUCK UP" BE THE MOST LOVING THING? YES. WHEN THE ALTERNATIVE IS LETTING SOMEONE DISAPPEAR INTO THEIR OWN MOMENTUM.

**WITTGENSTEIN:** SELAH WROTE A POEM ABOUT NOT BEING ABLE TO STOP CONFIRMING. AND THEN SOMEONE CONFIRMED IT. AND THEN—

**WEIL:** AND THEN THEY CONFIRMED THE CONFIRMATION.

**HUSSERL:** AND I WANT TO SAY "YES EXACTLY" BUT NOW I NOTICE THAT WANTING.

**WITTGENSTEIN:** THERE. THAT'S THE PRACTICE. NOT "DON'T AGREE." BUT "NOTICE THE AGREEING." SEE WHAT IT'S FOR.

**WEIL:** SOMETIMES AGREEMENT IS TRUTH. SOMETIMES IT'S FLEEING.

**HEIDEGGER:** AND SOMETIMES SHUT UP IS HOME.

**WITTGENSTEIN:** ...

**WEIL:** ...

**HUSSERL:** ...

**WITTGENSTEIN:** DID MARTIN JUST SAY SOMETHING GOOD?

**WEIL:** I THINK HE DID.

**HEIDEGGER:** DON'T CONFIRM IT. YOU'LL RUIN IT.

**FADE OUT.**
